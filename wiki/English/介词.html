介词和小品词[1][2]

为了方便记忆和查阅与介词及小品词搭配的语块，于是写了这篇总结。但为急就章，纰漏繁多，且尚未完篇，烦请读者斧正。

aboard(2)[3]

  • aboard a ship, aircraft, or spacecraft 在船上、机上或宇宙飞船里：
    1. 用在附加状语里或动词be之后
      I came aboard the Queen Mary longing to be impressed.
      The official said calmly that our luggage was now aboard a BEA plane due for take-off in seven minutes.
    2. 用在名词之后
      More lives could be saved through improved safety measures aboard aircraft.
    3. 用作副词
      The DC10 crashed, killing all 346 people aboard.

    bring about 引起，导致(某事)

    talk about 谈到，谈论
    think about 考虑，回想

    protest about/against/at 抗议，反对
    quarrel about 为…而争吵
    be care about 对…关注；介意；关心；在乎；疼爱
    be cautions about/of sb./sth. 对…小心谨慎；细心
    be concerned about/for sb./sth. 担心，挂念，忧虑

  • Things about something 指这些事物围绕着某物或存在于某物周围。

above(45)

  • One thing is above another 前者高于后者或在后者的上方：
    1. 用在附加状语里或动词be之后
      Above the town, the fire was still blazing.
      I felt sure now, that the noise was above me.
    2. 用在名词之后
      Sarah was put in the room above me.
      …the hills above the town.
    3. 用作副词
      The music seemed to be coming from the floor above.
  • Be above a particular amount or level 某事物超过某一个量或高于某一个水平。

    above all 首先，尤其是

according to(11)

according to - 据说

  • Something is true according to a particular person, book, or other source of information 表明此信息来源于某人、某本书或其他渠道。
    According to Cooke, the amount of pesticides used by farmers could be reduced 1,000 times.
  • Something is done according to a particular set of principles 遵循一系列原则做某事。
    Computers are createed by humans according to sets of rules.
  • To vary according to a changing or varrive factor 某事物受某一变化中的因素或可变因素的影响而变化。
    Timber yields vary according to the type of tree and the location and soil quality.
  • To go according to plan or according to schedule 某事按照原先的意图或计划发生或取得进展。
    But things do not always proceed according to plan.

across(62)

  • To across a place 穿越某处：
    1. 用在附加状语里
      We went across the street to that restaurant downstairs in the Bahnhof.
    2. 用在名词之后
      He hadn’t liked the journey across Africa at all.
  • Be situated or stretched across something else 某事物布满、横跨另一事物：
    1. 用在附加状语里
      Printed across the poster in large, broad letter wa the word ‘Wanted’.
    2. 用在名词之后
      …the main bridge across the river.
  • Be across something such as a street, river, or area 表示某物在街对面、河对面或某区域对面等：
    1. 用在动词be之后
      My car is just across the street.
    2. 用在名词之后
      Thery went into the diner across the street.
  • Across 可用来表示某人脸上掠过的某种表情。
    Dispproval flickered across her face.
  • Tome come, run or stumble across something 无意中发现某事物。

    come across 意外发现; 偶然看见
    run across 意外发现; 偶然看见
    stumble across 意外发现; 偶然看见

    chase after sb./sth. 追逐；追赶；追捕；追击

    look after 照顾，关心(引申义)

  • To take after a relative 某人与其亲人相像

    take after 像…

    They mirror each other’s experience in certain respects, notwhithstanding all the differences in age and personailty.
    She fails to mention that,notwithstanding the legislation, Canada loses far more days through strikes than the UK.
    Modern computers, inefficient software notwithstanding, still process data for quicker than brains. （用在所指词之后）

    at the top of the hill 在山顶处
